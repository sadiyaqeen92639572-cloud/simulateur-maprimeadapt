Here is a highly optimized prompt designed specifically for an AI search engine like Perplexity (or ChatGPT with web search enabled).
This prompt forces the AI to act as a Market Researcher, scour the web for what competitors are doing, find the "unanswered questions" on forums (like Reddit/Quora), and translate those gaps into high-converting quiz ideas.

📋 COPY & PASTE THIS PROMPT INTO PERPLEXITY:

Role: Act as an expert Market Researcher and Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Strategist specializing in interactive lead generation.
Objective: I want to build interactive quizzes/simulators for my website in the following niche: 

[  La Niche "Productivité IA & Automatisation" (B2B Solopreneurs)
Le gap entre "connaître ChatGPT" et "automatiser son business" est énorme.

Le Gap : Les freelances savent qu'ils perdent du temps mais ne savent pas quel outil (Make, Zapier, Notion) choisir.

Le Simulateur : "Diagnostic : Combien d'heures pouvez-vous automatiser par semaine ?"

Questions : Métier, outils actuels, temps passé sur les emails/factures/réseaux sociaux.

Monétisation : Capture de leads pour du conseil en automatisation, vente de templates Notion, affiliation SaaS.

Pourquoi pas YMYL : C'est de l'efficacité opérationnelle. ]


Instead of generic quizzes, I want to capture leads by solving actual problems and answering complex questions that my competitors are ignoring.
Please execute the following 4 steps using your web search capabilities:
Step 1: Competitor Landscape Analysis
Search the web to analyze how businesses in this niche currently capture leads. What do their standard forms, calculators, or quizzes look like? Identify the "status quo" (e.g., are they just asking for a zip code and phone number? Are they using generic "Get a Quote" buttons?).
Step 2: Audience Pain Point & Gap Discovery
Search forums (Reddit, Quora), social media, and "People Also Ask" boxes to find the most confusing, overwhelming, or complex aspects of this niche for a beginner.
What are the specific scenarios where people say "It depends, but I don't know what it depends on"?
What are the hidden fears or decision-paralysis moments buyers face before requesting a quote?
Identify 3 to 4 specific "Content Gaps" where competitors fail to provide personalized, immediate clarity.
Step 3: Quiz Ideation (Bridging the Gap)
Based on the gaps identified in Step 2, propose 4 highly engaging, interactive quiz/simulator concepts. These should NOT feel like traditional lead-gen forms. They must feel like valuable, diagnostic tools that give the user an "Aha!" moment in exchange for their contact info.
Step 4: Output Format
For each of the 4 quiz ideas, format your response exactly like this:
🎯 Quiz Title: (Catchy, benefit-driven, e.g., "The 60-Second [Niche] Viability Test")
🕳️ The Gap Addressed: (Which specific consumer confusion or competitor failure does this solve?)
🪝 The Hook: (The compelling reason a user would click "Start Quiz")
🧠 3-4 Key Questions to Ask: (Give examples of strategic questions that segment the user and build the algorithm)
🎁 The "Aha!" Result: (What personalized insight does the user get at the end that makes them willing to enter their email/phone number to see the full report or get a quote?)


💡 Why this prompt works perfectly for Perplexity:
It forces live research: By asking it to look at competitors and Reddit/Quora, Perplexity will actually search the web for current sentiments rather than relying on generic training data.
It focuses on "Decision Paralysis": The best quizzes don't just ask for data; they help the user make a difficult decision. Step 2 forces the AI to find out what is difficult about your niche.
It provides a strict output format: You get exactly what you need to plug into the quizzes.ts file of your React application (Title, Questions, and the Logic/Result).
Example of what it might generate for "Solar Panels":
Instead of a boring "Get a Solar Quote" quiz, it will find that people are confused about roof angles and shading, and might suggest a quiz titled: "The Solar Viability Score: Is your specific roof actually profitable?"
